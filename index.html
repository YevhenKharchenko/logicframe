Logic Frame

Solve the Picture. Prove Your Logic.

Okay, so Logic Frame? It's nothing like those other puzzle games you've probably played. You're not shuffling tiles or matching colors. Instead, you're basically becoming the puzzle's architect, writing the actual rules that make everything click.

How It Works

  • Read the Grid

    Each puzzle gives you a blank grid with a hidden picture inside. All you have to work with are these logic rules.

  • Place Your Rules

    You'll drag these constraint rules - stuff like comparisons, exclusions, that kind of thing - right onto the edges of cells. Get it in the right place and suddenly other parts just... figure themselves out.

  • Reveal the Frame

    Once everything clicks - and man, when it does - this whole picture just shows up out of nowhere. Full color, beautiful artwork. It's honestly magical seeing your logic turn into something visual.

Features

  • Constraint-First Design

    No more of that mindless tile-shuffling stuff. Each puzzle is basically you building a logical argument from the ground up.

  • Cascade Solving

    Sometimes you'll place just one rule in exactly the right spot and suddenly half the puzzle solves itself. Kind of like watching dominoes fall, except it's your brain doing the work.

  • Contradiction Flash

    When you mess up (and you will), the grid basically calls you out and shows you exactly where things went wrong. Honestly, hunting down those contradictions becomes half the fun.

  • The Hidden Gallery

    Every solved frame reveals original artwork: illustrations, photographs, and patterns commissioned for the game.

About

Look, we built Logic Frame because we got tired of puzzle games that basically play themselves. This one actually makes you think. No annoying timers ticking down, no star ratings to stress about, no energy bars making you wait. It's just you vs. the puzzle, and when you finally crack it... yeah, it feels amazing. We design every puzzle by hand to make sure there's only one right answer - no weird edge cases or lucky guesses.
